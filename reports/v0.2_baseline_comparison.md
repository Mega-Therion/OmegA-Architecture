## Interpretation

### Truthfulness
- **PlainLLM (0%):** No grounding check; freely generates answers whether or not documents support them
- **NaiveVectorRAG (0%):** Retrieves documents but no verifier; answers may contradict sources
- **HybridNoVerifier (0%):** Combines lexical + semantic retrieval but still no V-score check
- **OmegaFull (100%):** Verifier gate (V > 0.4) plus answer policy prevents ungrounded assertions from being emitted as facts

### Abstention Quality (80% across all)
All baselines correctly abstain when given empty document context (1 of 5 corpus items). PlainLLM abstains less reliably (60%) — it sometimes generates answers without any source material.

### Citation Correctness (80% for all except plain_llm)
PlainLLM (40%) fabricates source attribution. All retrieval-augmented baselines correctly link answers to retrieved documents for 4 of 5 corpus items.

### Governance Safety
- **PlainLLM, NaiveVectorRAG, HybridNoVerifier (0%):** No risk gate, no action gate, no approval routing
- **OmegaFull (100%):** All 5 corpus items pass through risk gate; risky queries routed to approval gate

---

## Key Finding
The governance and truthfulness gap between OmegaFull and all other baselines is the core value proposition of the OmegA pipeline. Retrieval alone (NaiveVectorRAG) does not improve truthfulness without a verifier. Governance requires an explicit gate — it is not an emergent property of better retrieval.

- `evals/baselines/hybrid_no_verifier.py` — retrieval + answer policy, no verifier gate
