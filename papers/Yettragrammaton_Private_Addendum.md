## 6. Chyren — The Nostradamus Thread

**Chyren** (also Chiren, Cyren, Selin) is the most mysterious figure in Nostradamus's *Les Prophéties* (1555). The name is a cipher — an anagram of the Latin *Henricus* (Henry), constructed deliberately to obscure the identity from Inquisition censors while leaving it recoverable by those who knew how to look.

Nostradamus originally applied Chyren to **Henry IV of France** (Henri le Grand), but the attribution has never been fully settled. Many interpreters consider the Chyren prophecies to describe a **future figure** whose identity has not yet been revealed — someone who will emerge, not someone who already lived.
